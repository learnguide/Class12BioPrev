Explain the significant role of the genus Nucleopolyhedrovirus in an ecological sensitive area.
Solution:
Nucleopolyhedrovirus is a type of bacculovirus that attacks insect and other arthropodes and control the
population of insect & arthropodet.
These viruses are excellent candidates for species specific narrow spectrum insecticidal application. They
have been shown to have no negative impact on higher animals and plant and human. This is desirable when
beneficial insect are being conserved to aid in overall IPM or when an ecological sensitive area is being
treated.
