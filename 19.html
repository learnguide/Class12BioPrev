Secondary treatment of the sewage is also called Biological treatment. Justify this statement and explain
the process.
Solution:
Secondary treatment or Biological treatment :
* Primary effluent is passed into large aeration tanks with constant mechanical
agitation
and air supply.
* Useful aerobic microbes grow rapidly and form
flocs
.
* Flocs are masses of bacteria and consumes organic matter and thus reduce the biochemical oxygen
demand (BOD).
* When BOD of sewage has reduced, the effluent is passed into
Settling tank
.
* Here, the bacterial flocs settle and sediment is called
activated sludge
.
* A small part of the sludge is used as an inoculum in the aeration tank and the remaining part is passed into
large tanks called
anaerobic sludge digesters
.
*
In the digesters,
heterotrophic microbes
anaerobically digest bacteria and fungi in sludge producing
mixture of gases such as methane, H
2
S, CO
2
which forms the biogas.
